# Concierge conversation — 10 design studies (C1–C10)

**How to review:** in the preview, use the `‹ ›` switcher below the phone to
flip between `C1 · Page` → `C10 · Compare`. All ten render the same fixture —
"Somewhere warm in October — a week with the girls" → a Peru / Oaxaca /
Alentejo shortlist — so differences are purely *form*. Every study obeys the
system: de-bubbled concierge voice, hairline user blocks, narration not
spinners, paper = concierge material, pills = instruments, 4px squares =
content, no accent graphics.

They sit on a spectrum from **canvas-dominant** (C3) to **chat-dominant**
(C5/C8), with hybrids between.

---

## The ten studies

**C1 · Page** — the baseline. Typeset reply + full-width editorial cards
stacked in the scroll; staggered arrival. *Best at:* first impressions,
magazine feel. *Weak at:* long conversations (cards push history away).

**C2 · Sheet** — conversation as a paper sheet docked over a masonry canvas
(Kayak split-attention, calmed). The canvas is the screen; chat rides on it.
*Best at:* keeping inventory visible while talking. *Weak at:* transcript
depth; the sheet can only hold the latest beat. (Research Recipe 1 suggests
detents — peek/half/full — as the real interaction.)

**C3 · Spread** — one destination per full-bleed page; the concierge's case
typeset on a scrim; swipe between spreads, dots for the shortlist, floating
composer. *Best at:* seduction, portfolio stills, "look closer" moments.
*Weak at:* comparison and multi-turn memory. Strongest as a *state* you tap
into from any other variant, not the whole experience.

**C4 · Brief** — Artifacts lineage: a pinned, versioned TRIP BRIEF (when/who/
vibe) that the conversation edits; shortlist as compact document rows. *Best
at:* making progress tangible; the group-shareable object; long planning arcs.
*Weak at:* romance — it's the least dreamy. (Research Recipe 4's collapsing
"masthead" is the evolution: brief as header that shrinks on scroll.)

**C5 · Transcript** — chat-first: two full turns visible, imagery embedded as
inline horizontal strips inside replies (never heroes). Burst rhythm from
iMessage; the back-and-forth is the point. *Best at:* conveying fluid
conversation; history legibility. *Weak at:* image impact; deciding.

**C6 · Board** — two named surfaces: Chat stays pure; everything visual
accrues to a Board (moodboard masonry); an inline paper receipt ("Added to
your board") stitches them. *Best at:* separation of thinking and looking;
group shareability of the board. *Weak at:* split attention — flipping is a
mode switch. (Tabs verified working.)

**C7 · Voice** — the editorial voice mode: your words form as a large
pull-quote on a white wash; the canvas surfaces results *while you speak*;
"Listening" written, not animated; "Tap to type instead" always present. No
orb, ever. *Best at:* the brand's voice-mode signature (unclaimed territory
per research); hands-busy use. *Weak at:* dense information.

**C8 · Briefing** — Perplexity's structure, editorialized: sectioned answer
(OCTOBER BRIEFLY / THE SHORTLIST ranked with tabular figures / WORTH KNOWING)
plus sources-are-people attribution row. *Best at:* information-first users;
trust display; scannability. *Weak at:* warmth; imagery.

**C9 · Rail** — the research favorite (Kayak's measured insight: people
refine in chat but decide from a live, browsable set). Transcript scrolls; a
LIVE SHORTLIST rail persists above the composer, and constraints visibly act
on it — "under 6 hours" dims Peru with the reason attached ("set aside ·
9h 40m"). *Best at:* the refine→decide loop; showing the agent listening.
*Weak at:* rail real estate on long content.

**C10 · Compare** — the decision canvas: three candidates in columns, facts
in aligned tabular rows, per-row best marked by weight, then one line of
judgment ("My pick for five: Peru"). Group-aware steers ("The group votes").
*Best at:* settling arguments; previews screen 7's hotel-vs-house pattern.
*Weak at:* early exploration.

## Research digest (chat-apps deep dive, 2026-07-05)

Full findings in the session; what matters most for us:

- **The transcript is a magazine being composed in real time.** De-bubbled
  replies should use the real type scale (kicker → prose → module →
  provenance); streaming = pagination, blocks settle as composed units.
- **Burst rhythm** (iMessage): metadata once per *turn*, never per message;
  generous space between turns, tight within.
- **Closed module vocabulary** (the anti-generative-UI rule): the concierge
  composes replies only from a fixed set — Place card, Day plan, Compare
  grid, Confirm slip, Poll, Map snippet. 3–5 items per set, one horizontal
  scroll region per reply max. Consistent modules = "a friend with consistent
  handwriting."
- **Swipe-to-quote** (WhatsApp): swipe any card/paragraph to "hold it up" to
  the concierge — quoted thumbnail docks above the composer. Referential
  conversation without threads.
- **Reactions as approvals** (tapbacks): long-press a proposal → save/love/
  veto; companions' reactions as tiny avatar dots on the card corner. The
  group consensus mechanic disguised as a tapback.
- **Voice inside the thread** (ChatGPT 2025): live transcript in place,
  visuals surfacing inline while speaking — never a takeover. C7 is our
  version.
- **Chips discipline:** máx 3 follow-ups, latest turn only, gone on scroll.
- **Anti-patterns to hold the line on:** wall-of-text replies; per-message
  chrome; chip litter; unbounded generative layouts; keyboard/takeover sins.

## Synthesis recommendation

The ten aren't really competitors — they're **phases of one conversation**
plus a **module vocabulary**:

1. **Base rhythm:** C1's page (voice + modules in the scroll), with C5's
   burst spacing rules for multi-turn history.
2. **Persistent state:** C4's brief as a collapsing masthead (Recipe 4) —
   progress always visible, tap to expand.
3. **Refine→decide:** C9's live rail appears when a browsable set exists;
   C10's compare grid is an inline module when the question is "which one?".
4. **Look closer:** C3's spread as the full-screen state of any place card.
5. **Voice:** C7's grammar whenever the mic is used.
6. **Group layer:** tapback-approvals on any proposal module (from research).

That composite — one conversation that changes form by phase — is itself the
portfolio story: "the concierge doesn't send messages; it typesets whatever
the moment needs."
