# Screen studies — 24 variants across 6 screen families

Companion to `concierge-variants.md` (C1–C10). **How to review:** flip the
`‹ ›` switcher below the phone. Order: C1–C10 → P1–P4 (profile) → I1–I4
(itinerary) → F1–F4 (flights) → S1–S4 (stay) → A1–A4 (approval) → VS1–VS4
(voice seats). All obey the system: theme tokens only, no accent, two shapes,
paper = concierge material, de-bubbled voice, tabular figures, trust = faces.

---

## Profile — "the travel passport" (P1–P4)

**P1 · Passport** — the profile as a collected identity artifact (Airbnb '25 ×
Retro): stamp-chip preferences with designed ghost "add" invitations, trips as
filmstrip rows, people as named avatar clusters. *Notice:* strips bleed off
the right edge while captions hold the 16px axis; beautiful at 40% complete.
*Tradeoff:* densest — nothing gets a hero moment.

**P2 · Journal** — the contributor page: identity as authorship. Name + bio
set large, collections as collaged board covers, trips as quiet rows,
preferences demoted to one chevron row. *Notice:* taste expressed through
curation, not settings. *Tradeoff:* the agent-powering preference data becomes
a footnote.

**P3 · Interview** — preference capture as conversation on the concierge's
paper: answered questions collapse to stamp rows, the live question offers
pill options (Duolingo/Spotify grammar); "The more I know, the less you plan."
*Notice:* progress felt via the accumulating stack — no bar. *Tradeoff:* a
moment, not a destination; needs P1/P2 behind it.

**P4 · Trust** — the autonomy dial: permissions as plain-language sentences
with a right-aligned state word (On / Ask first), grouped BOOKINGS / CHANGES /
SPENDING; the hero is the earned-trust proposal ("I've gotten 3 bookings
right…") with [Allow] vs a quieter "Keep asking." *Notice:* not a single
switch component on screen. *Tradeoff:* all typography; least photographic.

## Itinerary artifact (I1–I4)

**I1 · Issue** — the draft as a magazine issue: full-bleed cover ("Peru",
white tabular meta), days as spreads with typeset prose instead of list rows;
closing colophon: "Drafted from 6 saves and Maya's trip — tell me what to
change." *Tradeoff:* seductive but slow to scan; times buried in sentences.

**I2 · Timeline** — the conversational middle ground: voice intro + sourcing
narration, then the signature day-dots rail (ink dots, hairline connectors,
tabular times, square thumbs, Maya's chip on Maras). *Tradeoff:* the most
conventional — a very good list.

**I3 · Scrubber** — the versioned artifact: v1 · v2 · v3 chips (v3 active), a
plain-language change note ("v3 — swapped the museum for Maras, per Maya"),
"changed" hairline tags on moved lines. *Notice:* change attribution is a
person, not a diff. *Tradeoff:* spends its top on history; strongest
mid-negotiation, weakest as first reveal.

**I4 · Blocks** — the multiplayer draft: days as paper cards whose lines are
proposals — approved in full ink with avatar dots, pending ghosted at 0.45
with "2 of 5" tags; "3 approved · 4 awaiting the group." *Tradeoff:* ghosting
sacrifices legibility of exactly the items needing attention; wants the
develop animation.

## Flights + points (F1–F4)

**F1 · Board** — the departure board (Flighty): one line per flight, 16pt
tabular times, price at the right edge; the pick marked only by weight + a
profile-citing rationale. *Tradeoff:* presents rather than persuades.

**F2 · Points** — the points desk: paper card with the PAY comparison ($612
cash vs 45k MR → Avios, "~1.4¢/pt · saves $180"), then intent-preview steps
and [Approve the plan] + "I'll handle it myself." *Notice:*
consent-before-consequence as typography, not a modal. *Tradeoff:* assumes the
flight's chosen — beat two of the flow.

**F3 · PickFirst** — the recommendation: one hero card with the route-line viz
at full size, three check-marked reasons citing the group and profile;
alternatives collapsed to muted one-liners. *Notice:* confidence expressed by
order and weight. *Tradeoff:* least browsable; comparison lives behind trust.

**F4 · Group** — five seats, one fare: traveler rows (avatar · preference
honored · seat in medium tabular), one fare line ("$612 × 5 · $3,060 · splits
via the trip"), approvals as 2 ink + 3 ghosted avatars. *Tradeoff:* no
alternatives; depends on F1/F3 upstream.

## Hotel vs house (S1–S4)

**S1 · Duel** — two columns, needs as aligned fact rows (BEDS / QUIET /
MORNINGS / SPA / PP·NIGHT), better answer per row in medium ink; verdict names
Rosa and converts the price gap into "$86 a night back." *Tradeoff:* images
squeezed to evidence size — never seduction.

**S2 · Needs** — the brief leads: THE BRIEF, FOR FIVE — five need rows
answered "Casa / Hotel / Both," properties demoted to thumb rows; one pill
[Hold the casa]. *Notice:* the screen argues itself to a single action.
*Tradeoff:* least visual; gives up the magazine moment.

**S3 · Story** — the concierge advocates each stay in turn (spread + typeset
case, Tomo's chip on the casa), then rules from its own paper — the material
change marks advocacy → judgment. *Tradeoff:* longest scroll; persuades
sequentially, not simultaneously.

**S4 · Votes** — consensus as a designed object: five avatar dots per option
(ink = voted, ghosted = pending), tabular scoreboard ("Casa 2 · Hotel 1 · 2
open"), concierge deliberately neutral ("Nudge them / Decide for us / Wait").
*Notice:* state, urgency, and social pressure with zero color. *Tradeoff:*
presumes the duel already happened.

## Proactive approval (A1–A4)

**A1 · Slip** — the definitive intent-preview: context voice line, the plan as
a paper slip (numbered steps on hairline rules, tabular times), rationale,
"Held for 18 more minutes," group dots, [Approve] + two quiet outs. *Notice:*
urgency is a tabular line, never a countdown. *Tradeoff:* safest; no view of
what the itinerary becomes.

**A2 · Push** — the full proactive arc in one still: the quiet lock-screen
push (no specifics) above the expanded plan card; white/hairline vs paper =
two materials, two moments. *Tradeoff:* a diagram of a flow — exists to sell
the arc, not to ship.

**A3 · Develop** — approval as visual state on the itinerary itself: current
items ghosted/struck, proposed items full ink with NEW tags and developing
dots — the before/after diff IS the intent preview. *Tradeoff:* richest
spatial story, slowest read; heavy if a change spans days.

**A4 · Thread** — the same moment at conversation scale: narration, short
voice, the slip compacted to a tight module, consent via steers ("Approve ·
Keep Isolina · Ask the group") — no big pill. *Notice:* approving sits at the
same weight as declining. *Tradeoff:* least ceremonial; the consequential
action loses its 44pt prominence.

## Voice seats (VS1–VS4)

**VS1 · Map** — the live map: pull-quote ask + written "Working — holding row
15," the cabin as squares (yours ink, proposed developing at 45%, EXIT tag),
delta line, [Keep these seats]. *Notice:* the ghosted seats ARE the progress
indicator — no orb needed. *Tradeoff:* vertically loaded.

**VS2 · Quote** — the transcript is the interface: both sides typeset,
before/after seat strips (muted scatter → settled ink block), tabular delta.
*Notice:* fastest read of the four. *Tradeoff:* no map = no spatial
verification.

**VS3 · Slip** — the change slip: hairline card, paper FLIGHT header, five
traveler rows (struck old seat → medium new seat), "+$36 · split 5 ways ·
$7.20 each." *Notice:* the group-travel thesis in one line; screenshot-able
into the group chat. *Tradeoff:* least "voice" of the four.

**VS4 · Ambient** — the ambient bar: the full cabin owns the screen; the
conversation compresses to a slim paper ribbon (transcript one-liner,
"Listening," [End]). *Notice:* the closest reading of "voice as a layer over
the task." *Tradeoff:* the resting state, not the decision moment.

## Cross-cutting observations for review

1. **Paper discipline held everywhere:** across all 24, `surfaceMuted` only
   ever means "the concierge is holding this" — slips, proposals, interview
   cards, verdict cards, voice ribbons.
2. **The develop pattern recurs** (I4, F4, S4, A3, VS1): provisional opacity →
   ink is becoming the app's consent language. Worth blessing formally as a
   component (`ApprovalCluster`) after review.
3. **Weight-as-recommendation** (F1, S1, C10) works: medium vs regular reads
   clearly as "the pick" without a single hue.
4. **Each family has one editorial, one structural, one conversational, and
   one multiplayer take** — so cross-family composites are easy to assemble
   (e.g., I1's cover + I2's rail + I4's dots).
